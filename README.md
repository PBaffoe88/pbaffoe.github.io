# Featured Research

## 🏠 The Childcare Constraint
**Working Paper — Preparing for Submission**

**Estimating the Causal Effect of Childcare Access on Labor Supply in Central Minnesota**

### Research Question

Does access to licensed childcare increase labor-force participation, employment, and full-time work among parents of young children?

### Highlights

- Integrated 20 years of Minnesota childcare licensing records with ACS PUMS microdata.
- Estimated causal effects using Difference-in-Differences, Instrumental Variables, and Regression Discontinuity.
- Found heterogeneous responses across household types, with contrasting effects for single mothers and single fathers.
- Built a fully reproducible R research pipeline.

**Data**

- ACS PUMS
- Minnesota DHS/DCYF
- Minnesota DEED
- QCEW
- FRED

**Methods**

- Difference-in-Differences
- HonestDiD
- Instrumental Variables
- Regression Discontinuity

**Software**

R (fixest, rdrobust, ivreg, did)

## 🏭 Buffer Depletion and Price Instability
**Working Paper**

### The Inventory Channel of Monetary Policy in U.S. Manufacturing

**Research Question**

Do interest-rate shocks affect inventory behavior and producer-price volatility?

### Highlights

- Estimated local projections following contractionary monetary policy shocks.
- Identified persistent inventory accumulation in inventory-intensive industries.
- Evaluated producer-price volatility following monetary tightening.
- Performed placebo and split-sample robustness tests.

**Data**

- Census M3
- FRED
- BLS Producer Price Index
- San Francisco Fed Monetary Policy Surprises

**Methods**

- Jordà Local Projections
- Two-Way Fixed Effects
- Robustness Analysis

**Software**

R

## 🛢 Oil Prices and Geopolitical Risk
**Working Paper + Published Article**

### What 35 Years of Data Reveal About Oil Markets

**Research Question**

Does geopolitical uncertainty influence crude-oil prices or volatility?

### Highlights

- Estimated two-state Markov-switching models.
- Identified high-volatility crisis regimes.
- Estimated cumulative impulse responses using Jordà local projections.
- Evaluated oil-price momentum across multiple investment horizons.

**Data**

- Caldara–Iacoviello Geopolitical Risk Index
- WTI Spot Prices
- Macroeconomic Indicators

**Methods**

- Markov Switching
- EGARCH
- Local Projections
- Time-Series Momentum

**Software**

R

## 🌎 Spillover Effects of U.S. Fiscal Policy on OECD Monetary Policy
**M.S. Thesis**

### Research Question

How do U.S. fiscal-policy shocks influence monetary-policy decisions across OECD economies?

### Highlights

- Examined over thirty OECD countries.
- Estimated structural fiscal-policy shocks using SVAR.
- Measured heterogeneous international monetary-policy responses.
- Combined SVAR with country-level ARDL estimation.

**Data**

- OECD Statistics
- FRED
- National Accounts

**Methods**

- SVAR
- ARDL
- Cointegration
- Impulse Response Functions
- Forecast Error Variance Decomposition

R and Stata
